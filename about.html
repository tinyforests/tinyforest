About Us

Gardener & Son

Tyson & Natasha — Co-Founders, Garden Makers, Storytellers


Gardener & Son was founded by Tyson and Natasha, partners in life and creative vision, who share a belief that gardens can be both ecological and deeply human. Together, they've shaped one of Melbourne's most distinctive ecological garden design studios — where science, story, and design come together to restore beauty and biodiversity.

Tyson — Ecological Designer & Land Reader

Tyson approaches every landscape through the lens of custodianship, biophilic experience, and human health. He designs with the understanding that gardens should restore people as much as they restore place.

His work is anchored in regenerative practice, the circular economy, and a deep respect for how healthy ecosystems function — creating landscapes that elevate wellbeing and feel deeply connected to Country.

Where Tyson shapes the land, Natasha brings it to life.

Natasha

Plant Stylist, Palette Maker, Aesthetic Visionary

Natasha designs the plant palettes, plant combinations, and spatial planting rhythms that define each Gardener & Son project. Her intuition for colour, foliage texture, seasonal expression, and the emotional tone of a space is unmatched.

In the studio, she creates environments that feel warm, soulful, and curated. In the garden, she extends that same aesthetic into living form — crafting plant communities that are not only beautiful but ecologically functional, climate-responsive, and generous to wildlife.

Together — A Studio Built on Ecology, Design, and Story

Tyson and Natasha blend their gifts to create gardens that are immersive, biodiverse, culturally grounded, and genuinely regenerative. Their work is defined by:

  • respect for Indigenous ecologies and Country
  • a commitment to biodiversity and climate resilience
  • a belief in gardens as places of meaning and memory
  • a design style that is naturalistic, soulful, and deeply place-based

Their vision is simple: To make biodiversity mainstream — one garden, one story, one community at a time.

At Gardener & Son, we acknowledge the Wurundjeri Woi Wurrung people of the Kulin Nation, the Traditional Custodians of the land on which we live and work. We honour their enduring connection to Country — and the grasslands, woodlands, and wetlands that once flourished here through generations of care and balance. These living systems were not lost to time, but to disconnection. Our work seeks to rekindle that relationship, restoring native ecologies and weaving biodiversity back into the urban fabric — each garden a quiet act of remembrance and renewal.
